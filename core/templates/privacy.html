Overview & Contact

This Policy explains how we collect, use, and share information when you visit our website, request an appointment, or make a payment. For questions or privacy requests, email attorneylydia@gmail.com.

Data We Collect

We collect:

  • Identifiers & contact details (name, email, phone).
  • Appointment details (preferred times, notes—please avoid sensitive/confidential info).
  • Payment information (processed by our payment processor; we receive limited transaction metadata).

Sources of Personal Information

Directly from you (forms/booking), automatically via our site (logs/cookies), and from service providers (e.g., payments).

How We Use Personal Information

  • Schedule and manage appointments; communicate with you.
  • Process payments and maintain records.
  • Operate, secure, and improve the website and services.
  • Comply with legal and ethical obligations.

Sharing & Disclosures

We share information with service providers that help us operate the site and services (hosting, payments, scheduling/calendar, email/SMS, analytics). We do not sell personal information. If we later implement advertising technologies that constitute “sharing,” we will add an opt-out mechanism.

Payments

Payments are processed by Stripe. We do not store full card numbers on our systems. Stripe uses industry-standard security (PCI DSS Level 1).

Calendar Integrations

If you connect a calendar to schedule/manage appointments, use of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. We request the minimum necessary scopes.

Cookies & Online Tracking

We use cookies and similar technologies for essential site functionality and to understand site usage. Browser “Do Not Track” signals may not be recognized by all parts of the site. If we implement advertising/remarketing technologies, we will update this section and honor Global Privacy Control (GPC) where required.

Children’s Privacy

Our site is not directed to children, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

Your Choices & Rights

You may request access, correction, or deletion of certain personal information by emailing us. We will verify and respond as required by law.

Security

We employ administrative, technical, and physical safeguards appropriate to our practice. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure.

Retention & Location

We retain information as necessary for the purposes above and as required by law and professional obligations. Data is processed in the United States.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. We will post the updated version with a new “Last updated” date.
